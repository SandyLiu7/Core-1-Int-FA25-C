Rock Sugar Poached Pear

Instructions

1.Soak the white fungus (Tremella) for about 1 hour until fully expanded. Chop it into small pieces.

2.Prepare the Asian pear (peel and dice), remove pits from red dates and cut them open.

3.In a clay pot or saucepan, add the white fungus and enough cold water to cover. Bring to a boil. Stir a few times with chopsticks, then continue boiling on high heat for 10 minutes.

4.Add the pear and red dates. Reduce to medium-low heat and simmer for another 10 minutes, stirring occasionally. At this point, the soup should become slightly thick and gelatinous.

5.Turn off the heat, add the rock sugar, cover, and let it sit for 10 minutes until the sugar fully dissolves.

Tips: To avoid the soup becoming too thick, make sure to add enough water. Stir a few times after it starts boiling to prevent the fungus from sticking to the bottom.

Rock Sugar Poached Pear is a traditional Chinese sugar water. It has a refreshing and smooth taste, with a natural fruity aroma and a slightly sweet rock sugar fragrance. The main ingredient, pear, is cold in nature and can moisten the lungs, relieve coughing, generate body fluids and clear heat. Tremella fuciformis is rich in plant gelatin and has the effects of nourishing Yin, beautifying the complexion and moistening dryness. Red dates and wolfberries can tonify qi and nourish blood, and balance the overall cold and cool nature. The entire bowl of sugar water is smooth in the mouth and rich in gelatin, making it suitable for drinking in autumn and winter to relieve dryness and dry cough. It is not merely a dessert, but more like traditional "food therapy" - satisfying the taste buds while also emphasizing the balance and maintenance of the body.

Ingredients

1 Asian pear (peeled and diced)

1 piece of white fungus (Tremella), soaked in water for 1 hour and finely chopped

3–4 dried red dates (pitted and halved)

1 tablespoon goji berries (wolfberries)

30–40 g rock sugar

Water (enough to cover ingredients generously)
